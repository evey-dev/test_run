How the final argument is structured
------------------------------------

The report follows an evidence-building sequence:

1. Establish clean model competence and define decisive token contrasts.
2. Train the lightweight ReLU SAEs requested by the project specification.
3. Observe weak sparse interventions but strong broad activation controls.
4. Measure SAE reconstruction and sparsity rather than guessing at the failure.
5. Find high validation FVE but thousands of active ReLU latents.
6. Replace the ambiguous L1 sparsity mechanism with fixed TopK candidates and
   unit-normalised decoder columns.
7. Select TopK candidates from diagnostics before looking at intervention
   outcomes.
8. Test graph-derived arithmetic panels with matched no-carry controls; reject
   their carry-specificity interpretations.
9. Condition a new arithmetic screen on output digit; retain the failed Top-10
   primary, then freeze and replicate the secondary Top-20 hypothesis once.
10. Test units with a mass donor control and frozen discovery/confirmation
    split; obtain the strongest positive result.

This order records scientific progress without turning the report into a diary.
Each change answers a specific diagnostic problem.

Original ReLU SAE diagnosis
---------------------------

All SAEs have input width 2,560 and latent width 8,192. Each behaviour/layer
uses 800 training and 200 validation vectors. The initial ReLU objective was
MSE + 3e-4 times mean latent L1, trained for 75 epochs with Adam at 1e-3 and
batch size 64.

Mean validation diagnostics over seven layers:

  Addition: FVE 0.9545, L0 2,166.6, validation dead fraction 0.0106
  SI units: FVE 0.9591, L0 3,544.6, validation dead fraction 0.0000
  Capitals: FVE 0.9068, L0 3,102.8, validation dead fraction 0.0000

The important inference is not "reconstruction error caused failure". FVE is
high. The measured problem is that the ReLU representations are dense. High FVE
does not require semantic or causal separability.

Mathematical reason the original objective is weak as a sparsity guarantee:

  W_e' = c W_e, b_e' = c b_e, W_d' = W_d / c

for 0 < c < 1 preserves the ReLU reconstruction because z' = c z, while it
reduces the raw L1 term. Decoder norms were not constrained in the original
models. This does not prove optimisation used the degeneracy, but it proves the
penalty is not an invariant sparsity measure. The measured L0 values show the
practical density.


Arithmetic evidence
-------------------

Main teacher-forced example:

  Question: What is 58 + 83? Answer: 1

The next token is the tens digit of 141. Correct token 4 had logit 30.62;
dropped-carry token 3 had logit 23.50, so the clean gap was 7.12.

Exploratory hierarchy:

- Inhibiting 73 positive graph features reduced the gap only to 6.63.
- A strength-2 complete SAE-latent patch from 54+83 reduced it to 0.74.
- A raw all-position MLP patch transferred source digit 3.
- A 44+83 control transferred source digit 2, so broad patching transfers
  answer state, not an independently established no-carry variable.
- All-position MLP knockout reduced p(4) to 0.6406, raised p(3) to 0.1836 and
  p(2) to 0.1426. This is broad digit destabilisation, not a carry circuit.
- Layer 24 was impactful under destructive knockout, but this localises causal
  dependence only at the component level.

TopK candidate selection:

- Fixed candidates: k = 128, 256, 512.
- Required mean FVE >= 0.90, every layer FVE >= 0.85, and mean combined dead
  fraction <= 0.80.
- Choose the eligible candidate with smallest mean validation L0.
- k=128 failed dead fraction (0.8324).
- k=256 was selected: mean FVE 0.9410, minimum layer FVE 0.9319, mean L0 171.6,
  dead fraction 0.7162.

All-position matched benchmark, 12 pairs:

  carry target delta       -0.875, CI [-0.969, -0.771]
  no-carry control delta   -0.885, CI [-0.958, -0.823]
  target-control            0.010, CI [-0.125,  0.167]

Final-position frozen screen, 8 discovery and 24 confirmation pairs:

  top-10 target delta      -0.1406, CI [-0.1719, -0.1146]
  no-carry control delta   -0.1094, CI [-0.1458, -0.0729]
  target-control           -0.0313, CI [-0.0885,  0.0260]
  target moved negative in 21/24; no top-token flips

The TopK features affect arithmetic logits, but the control interval crosses
zero. The predeclared carry-specificity test failed. This is a controlled null
result, not a reason to relabel the panel after viewing it.

Output-digit-balanced localisation, 32 discovery and 32 confirmation pairs:

  raw MLP conditioned AUC L24/L28             1.0000 / 1.0000
  frozen top-10 activation conditioned AUC    1.0000
  frozen top-10 activation difference         +1.1297, CI [+1.0106, +1.2531]
  top-10 carry target delta                    -0.0078
  top-10 no-carry control delta                -0.0234
  top-10 paired specificity                    +0.0156, CI [-0.0352, +0.0625]
  predeclared top-10 success                   false

The balanced experiment shows that carry status is linearly decodable in late
raw MLP outputs and highly separable in the frozen SAE activation panel after
controlling for the tens digit. The predeclared sparse causal test nevertheless
failed: decodability is not evidence that those ten features form a selective
mechanism.

The secondary Top-20 panel had paired specificity -0.1016, CI
[-0.1484, -0.0508]. Because this panel was inspected alongside several panel
sizes after the Top-10 primary was declared, it was treated as hypothesis
generation. Its exact 20 IDs were frozen and tested once on 32 of the 41
eligible cases that had no previous feature intervention. The replication
passed: carry target -0.0586, CI [-0.0898, -0.0234]; no-carry control +0.0273,
CI [-0.0039, +0.0586]; paired specificity -0.0859, CI
[-0.1367, -0.0391]. There were no top-token flips. Stop panel search here.

The final arithmetic figure is
`report/figures/fig_math_balanced_carry_replication.pdf`, generated by
`python -m src.plot_math_carry_replication`. Describe the result as a
distributed carry-associated causal panel under one SAE basis and a case-level

Protocol choices not obvious from code alone
--------------------------------------------

- The units feature screen first used indirect wording and yielded too few
  clean, fully qualified systems. The prompt pool was changed before any
  feature intervention, not after viewing a causal result. The final direct
  wording was baseline-audited over 64 physical systems and four variants.
- Discovery and confirmation are split by physical system, not merely by prompt
  string, to reduce template leakage.
- Arithmetic needs a no-carry control because a decrease in the 4-vs-3 gap can
  reflect generic digit-state disruption.
- Units needs a mass donor because any factual-recall donor could otherwise be
  mistaken for a force-specific effect.
- Final screens use the final token because that matches SAE training support.
  Earlier all-position results remain exploratory distribution-mismatched
  controls.
- Decoder top-output labels are diagnostic evidence only. They do not name or
  prove the semantics of a latent.
- Bootstrap intervals use 5,000 seeded resamples and describe variation across
  constructed prompt systems, not model-sampling or SAE-seed uncertainty.


Infrastructure and fact-check
------------------------------

CSD3 became unavailable to the project for about two weeks during a late-June
heatwave. A University of Cambridge notice explicitly attributes the incident
to cooling failure during recent hot weather and lists CSD3 as unavailable. The
CSD3 status page later lists post-incident recovery. Working activation and
checkpoint artifacts were lost and regenerated. CSD3 documentation confirms
that hpc-work/RDS is not backed up and node-local files are transient.

Do not write that A100, L4 or H100 usage has a universal rate of 6.77 Colab
compute units per hour. Google's official FAQ says runtime limits, hardware
availability and usage conditions vary and are not published as fixed limits.
The accurate statement is that paid Colab runtime was compute-unit-budgeted,
which favoured targeted candidate sweeps and confirmation over multi-seed,
all-layer or CLT-scale training.

Recorded final notebook hardware includes T4 and NVIDIA A100-SXM4-80GB. There
is no retained evidence that an H100 was used for the reported final runs.
