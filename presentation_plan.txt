SPOKEN SCRIPT:

  replacement models of Claude. My implementation preserves the scientific
  workflow but not every architectural detail. It trains an independent sparse
  autoencoder on each selected MLP output in Qwen3-4B-Instruct, builds a
  contrastive attribution graph, and tests candidate features by inhibition or
  swap. This distinction matters. A cross-layer transcoder can model
  dependencies and reconstruction errors across layers directly; independent
  per-layer SAEs cannot. My results therefore test whether the workflow travels
  under the lightweight architecture required by the project, not whether I
  recreated Anthropic's internal model exactly."

SLIDE 6 - END-TO-END PIPELINE                                        1:10
========================================================================

TITLE:
  Independent, auditable reproduction pipeline

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  Prompts -> activations -> per-layer SAEs -> contrast graph -> causal test

  Discovery ranks features.
  Confirmation estimates the frozen panel's effect.

FIGURE:
  `presentation/figures/fig_presentation_reproduction_pipeline.pdf`
  Use the full figure at the largest readable size.

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "This is the complete analysis path. Synthetic prompt sets are generated
  deterministically. MLP outputs are captured at layers four, eight, twelve,
  sixteen, twenty, twenty-four and twenty-eight. An overcomplete SAE is trained
  separately at each layer. A contrastive attribution graph proposes candidate
  features. Discovery prompts rank those candidates, after which the panel is
  frozen and evaluated on disjoint confirmation prompts with a matched control
  and a bootstrap confidence interval. The lower branch is essential: broad
  MLP or full-latent edits are positive controls for information location, but
  only a sparse, specific and held-out effect supports a compact mechanism."

SLIDE 7 - SAE AND INTERVENTION MATHEMATICS                            1:35
========================================================================

TITLE:
  The learned basis and the exact edit

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  u_l = x_l / s_l
  a_l = ReLU(W_e(u_l - b_d) + b_e)
  z_l = TopK_k(a_l)
  u_hat_l = W_d z_l + b_d

  Inhibit S:  x'_l = x_l - s_l W_{d,S} z_{l,S}
  Swap S:     x'_l = x_l + s_l W_{d,S}(z^src_{l,S} - z^tgt_{l,S})

FIGURE:
  `presentation/figures/fig_presentation_sae_and_intervention.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "Let x sub l denote the MLP output at layer l and the edited token position,
  and let s sub l be its training scale. The encoder maps the normalised output
  into eight thousand one hundred and ninety-two candidate latents. Top-K keeps
  exactly k non-zero coordinates, and the decoder reconstructs the activation
  from unit-normalised feature directions. To inhibit a selected set S, I
  subtract only those decoded contributions from the original MLP output. To
  swap, I add the difference between the source and target feature
  contributions. In both cases the unselected part of the original activation
  remains intact. This makes the intervention a feature edit rather than a
  replacement by the full SAE reconstruction."


========================================================================
SLIDE 8 - ATTRIBUTION GRAPH                                          1:20
========================================================================

TITLE:
  A contrast graph is a local causal hypothesis

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  J(x) = ell_target(x) - ell_contrast(x)

  A_lj = z_lj * (partial J / partial z_lj)

  Arithmetic example: J = ell_4 - ell_3

  Straight-through splice:
  exact model output in the forward pass; SAE path in the backward pass.

FIGURE:
  `report/figures/fig_math_attribution_graph.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "A logit, written ell, is the unnormalised score assigned to a candidate next
  token before the softmax. I analyse a contrast J: the target logit minus a
  pre-specified alternative. The first-order attribution of feature j at layer
  l is its activation multiplied by the gradient of J with respect to that
  activation. Positive values locally support the target side of the contrast.
  I use a straight-through splice so that the forward value remains the exact
  model activation while gradients pass through the SAE representation. This
  prevents reconstruction error from changing the baseline merely because a
  graph was requested. The resulting pruned graph ranks candidates; it does not
  establish that those candidates are sufficient or specific."

SLIDE 9 - EVIDENCE STANDARD                                          1:15
========================================================================

TITLE:
  Three questions that must not be conflated

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  Reconstruction
  FVE = 1 - E[||x - x_hat||^2] / E[||x - mean(x)||^2]

  Decodability
  Can a held-out classifier recover the condition from the features?

  Causal specificity
  Does a frozen edit change the target contrast more than its matched control?

  Mechanistic claim = causal specificity on disjoint confirmation data

FIGURE:
  `presentation/figures/fig_presentation_evidence_standard.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "The central methodological distinction is shown here. Fraction of variance
  explained asks whether the SAE reconstructs its input. Held-out decodability
  asks whether the representation contains information about a condition. A
  causal test asks whether editing a frozen feature set changes the model's
  behaviour selectively. None of the first two logically implies the third. A
  representation can retain almost all variance while distributing one
  computation across many latents; a perfectly decodable label can also be a
  consequence or correlate of the computation. I therefore reserve
  mechanistic language for controlled intervention results."

SLIDE 19 - LIMITS OF THE REPRODUCTION                                1:20
========================================================================

TITLE:
  Where this reproduction differs from the target method

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  - Independent per-layer MLP SAEs, not a cross-layer transcoder
  - Seven selected layers; primary tests at the final token position
  - One model and one SAE seed per final configuration
  - No explicit cross-layer reconstruction-error nodes
  - Logit effects were often measurable without top-token transfer

  Consequence: absence of a compact panel is not absence of the computation.

FIGURE:
  None. Use a restrained two-column layout: design limit -> implication.

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "The main limitation is representational scope. Independent per-layer MLP
  SAEs do not model attention, residual-stream features or cross-layer error
  propagation in the way Anthropic's transcoder does. Seven layers and the
  final token position were selected to keep the reproduction lightweight and
  auditable. Final configurations used one seed, so seed stability is not
  established. The CSD3 cooling outage also required regeneration on finite
  Colab resources; after availability returned, I prioritised the ten-thousand-
  prompt scale controls over an exhaustive architecture-by-seed sweep. These
  constraints limit the strength and scope of the claims. They do not turn a
  null sparse intervention into evidence that the model lacks the computation."

BACKUP A - SYMBOLS AND INTERVENTION DEFINITIONS

TITLE:
  Mathematical definitions

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  x_l       MLP output at layer l and edited token position
  s_l       per-layer activation scale estimated on training data
  z_lj      activation of SAE feature j at layer l
  W_d[:,j]  decoder direction for feature j
  ell_t     pre-softmax logit for token t

  p(t|x) = exp(ell_t) / sum_v exp(ell_v)
  J = ell_target - ell_contrast
  A_lj = z_lj * partial J / partial z_lj

FIGURE:
  `presentation/figures/fig_presentation_sae_and_intervention.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "A logit is the model's unnormalised score for a token. Softmax converts the
  vector of logits into probabilities. I intervene on the MLP output x at a
  chosen layer and position. Each z coordinate weights one decoder direction.
  The contrast J avoids ranking features that merely raise every candidate
  digit or unit together. Its first-order feature attribution is activation
  times gradient."

BACKUP C - WHY THE ALL-POSITION KNOCKOUT DID NOT PRODUCE GIBBERISH

TITLE:
  Residual pathways preserve substantial computation

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  The edit removed selected MLP branch outputs only.
  It did not remove:
  - embeddings
  - attention branches
  - unedited transformer layers
  - residual connections

  Therefore degraded confidence, not random output, is the expected control.

FIGURE:
  `report/figures/fig_math_interventions.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "A full MLP knockout sounds like the entire model was deleted, but it was not.
  The intervention zeroed the MLP branch output at seven selected layers. Token
  embeddings, attention outputs, residual pathways and all other layers
  remained. The survival of a sensible answer is therefore expected. The
  confidence redistribution shows that the removed branches mattered without
  implying that they were the sole implementation of addition."


BACKUP D - WHY DECODED CARRY IS NOT YET A CARRY CIRCUIT

TITLE:
  Association, causation and sufficiency

ON-SCREEN TEXT:
  AUC = 1.0 establishes separability.
  It does not establish that the decoded direction is used by the model.

  Required causal comparison:
  delta_carry - delta_no-carry < 0
  with a confidence interval wholly below zero.

FIGURE:
  `report/figures/fig_math_balanced_carry_replication.pdf`

SPOKEN SCRIPT:
  "Carry and no-carry cases can be perfectly separated because the activation
  contains carry-correlated information. That information could be causal, a
  downstream record of the answer, or correlated with another property of the
  prompt. Inhibition tests whether the model uses the selected coordinates, and
  the matched no-carry subtraction tests specificity. The independently
  replicated Top-20 effect supports a causal contribution, but its small size
  and absence of output flips rule out a claim of sufficiency."

FINAL DELIVERY NOTES
========================================================================

1. Say "panel" for a selected set of SAE coordinates. Say "feature" only for
   one coordinate. Say "circuit" only when discussing the target publication
   or explicitly qualifying the claim.
2. Say "newtons" for the unit and "joules" for the unit; token-level plots may
   display the prefixes `new` and `j` because those are the model's first-token
   targets.
3. Do not say that the units panel makes the model answer "newtons". It changes
   the logit preference selectively, but the top answer remains "joules" in all
   sixteen confirmation cases.
4. Do not call the Top-20 arithmetic panel a pre-registered primary result. It
   was selected after the Top-10 result, then frozen and independently
   replicated. "Secondary, independently replicated" is the accurate phrase.
5. Do not say that a capital relation circuit was found unless the new 10k
   relation screen explicitly passes its stored primary success rule.
6. When asked whether more training data solved the problem, answer by domain:
   it materially strengthened the units panel; the new arithmetic and capital
   runs are separate scale controls and must be judged by their matched causal
   estimands, not their FVE alone.
7. Keep confidence intervals visible beside every headline causal effect.
8. Cite the target paper on Slide 3 and the project repository on Slide 20.
