How the project evolved
-----------------------

1. Original lightweight ReLU SAEs were trained independently at layers
   4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24 and 28 for math, units and capitals.

2. Initial feature inhibition and swaps looked weak. Intervention code was
   audited. It now supports:
   - error-preserving feature inhibition and swap-in;
   - complete latent swaps;
   - raw MLP activation patching;
   - MLP, attention and block knockout;
   - final/all/token-position selection;
   - layer scans and printed token positions;
   - pre-knockout activation norms;
   - graph feature sign filtering.

3. Final-token MLP knockout did little in arithmetic. All-position MLP knockout
   reduced p(4) to 0.6406 and raised p(3) to 0.1836, but also raised p(2) to
   0.1426. Layer 24 was especially disruptive. This localised broad causal
   dependence but did not isolate carry.

4. Broad all-position math swaps transferred source answer digits. A 54+83
   source moved 58+83 toward 3; a 44+83 source transferred 2. This showed that
   broad patches mainly transfer answer state rather than an abstract no-carry
   variable.

5. Unit complete-latent force-to-energy swapping produced a first-prefix flip
   in one minimal pair. Sparse graph swaps were much smaller. Capital swaps were
   weak even after high-confidence Zarqa/Amman and Basra/Baghdad controls.

6. SAE diagnostics were added. The original ReLU models have high validation
   FVE but very dense activity:
     math     FVE .9545, L0 2166.6
     units    FVE .9591, L0 3544.6
     capitals FVE .9068, L0 3102.8
   This ruled out simple reconstruction failure and motivated hard TopK.

7. `src/train.py` was extended with positive-ReLU TopK activation and optional
   unit-normalised decoder columns. TopK configurations use no L1 term.
   Candidate values were fixed at 128, 256 and 512. Selection uses diagnostics
   only: mean FVE >= .90, minimum layer FVE >= .85, mean combined dead fraction
   <= .80, then choose minimum mean L0.

8. Math selected TopK-256. It made sparse effects larger, but matched no-carry
   controls moved equally. A frozen top-10 graph screen on 24 confirmation
   pairs failed its predeclared specificity interval.

9. A separate output-digit-balanced arithmetic screen ranked all 57,344
   latents on 32 discovery pairs. Its Top-10 primary failed, but an exploratory
   Top-20 effect was frozen and replicated once on 32 intervention-untouched
   cases.

10. Units selected TopK-128. A final feature screen ranked 73 graph-positive
   features on 8 discovery physical systems and froze the top 10. It confirmed
   on 16 disjoint systems against a mass-source control.

11. CPU-only figure scripts generated publication-ready plots and compact
    Anthropic-like evidence diagram. No weights are required to regenerate
    these figures from JSON/CSV.

Important protocol history
--------------------------

The units feature screen initially used indirect wording. A baseline-only audit
found too few fully qualified systems. Before any feature interventions, the
pool was amended to direct "official SI unit used to measure X" wording, with
four variants over 64 physical systems. The final discovery/confirmation split
is by physical system and exact prompts are absent from the SAE corpus. This is
not post-outcome prompt selection.

All final feature screens use the final token because the SAEs were trained on
final-token MLP outputs. Earlier all-position results remain useful broad
controls but are out of distribution for the SAE.

Bootstrap confidence intervals use 5,000 seeded non-parametric resamples. They
quantify prompt-system variation only, not seed or model-sampling uncertainty.
