Math carry details
------------------

Main prompt:

  Question: What is 58 + 83? Answer: 1

This studies the tens digit of `141`.

Correct token:

  4

Dropped-carry alternative:

  3

Use contrast attribution:

  --target "4" --contrast-target "3"

Clean baseline:

  logit("4") = 30.62
  prob("4") ~= 1.0000
  logit("3") = 23.50
  prob("3") = 0.0008
  gap 4 - 3 = 7.12

Final-token MLP knockout:

  logit("4") increased to 33.25
  logit("3") stayed 23.50
  output stayed "4"

Interpretation:

Final-token MLP features are not the localized causal carry mechanism.

All-position MLP knockout:

  prob("4") fell to 0.6406
  logit("4") fell to 27.25
  prob("3") rose to 0.1836
  logit("3") rose to 26.00
  prob("2") rose to 0.1426
  gap 4 - 3 fell from 7.12 to 1.25

Interpretation:

This is the main positive math result. The carry-relevant state is affected by
broad MLP/residual updates across prompt positions. It is not a clean sparse SAE
carry circuit because other digit alternatives also rise.

Sparse SAE interventions:

They weakly and directionally move `3` upward, but do not flip the output. Report
this as partial evidence, not as a clean reproduction.


Can the carry feature be isolated?
----------------------------------

Not cleanly with current results.

The activation-level signal exists, but sparse graph-selected SAE features do not
control it on their own. Possible reasons:

- distributed representation
- nonlinear feature interactions
- relevant signal in SAE reconstruction error
- selected layers miss part of the computation
- final-token-only edits were too narrow
- very large clean confidence margin
- attention/residual pathways matter too

This is a limitation of the current SAE setup, not a proof that SAEs cannot ever
find carry features.

Final validation extension
--------------------------

New recommended final step:

  run_gpu_final_validation.ipynb

This notebook restores the final math, units and capitals checkpoints and the
fixed math/units graph JSONs. It does not retrain or rebuild a graph. It:

- recaptures deterministic final-position activation bundles only when missing;
- runs `src/sae_diagnostics.py` on original train/validation splits;
- runs `src/heldout_validation.py` on 12 graph-held-out arithmetic pairs and 12

- generates `fig_sae_diagnostics`, `fig_heldout_generalisation` and
  `fig_math_attribution_graph` in PDF and PNG form;

Interpret reconstruction results conditionally. Poor FVE can support
reconstruction failure as one contributor; good FVE rules out that simple
explanation. Neither result alone identifies the cause of sparse-intervention
failure. The old error-preserving reconstruction baseline is a forward identity,
not a fidelity metric.

The arithmetic benchmark pairs are absent from the SAE corpus. Unit sources are
selected from the SAE validation split and their matched energy targets are
absent from the SAE corpus. All benchmark pairs are graph-held-out, but unit
sources are not fully SAE-held-out.

Optional TopK follow-up
-----------------------

`run_gpu_math_topk_retrain.ipynb` is a guarded mathematics-only follow-up to the
diagnosis that the final ReLU SAEs reconstruct well but activate thousands of
latents. It trains TopK k=128/256/512 candidates with unit decoder norms, selects
the sparsest candidate meeting fixed FVE/dead-feature thresholds before any
intervention, builds one new graph and reruns the same 12 held-out pairs.

The benchmark additionally inhibits the graph features on each matched no-carry
source. A more-negative carry-target effect than no-carry control supports carry
specificity; equal effects support generic arithmetic-answer influence.

No old checkpoint or output is overwritten. If no candidate passes selection,
the notebook backs diagnostics up to Drive and stops before graph construction.


Completed TopK result and next arithmetic run
----------------------------------------------

The TopK notebook has now been run. Metric-only selection chose k=256. Mean
validation FVE is 0.9410 (minimum layer 0.9319), mean validation L0 is 171.6 and
mean dead-feature fraction is 0.716. This is a real sparsity improvement over the
original dense ReLU SAE codes.

The sparse held-out carry effect strengthened from -0.396 to -0.875 in
correct-minus-dropped-carry logit-gap delta, but the matched no-carry control was
-0.885. The paired target-minus-control estimate is +0.010 with bootstrap 95% CI
[-0.125, +0.167]. Therefore the completed TopK result does not isolate carry;
the graph feature group has a generic arithmetic-answer effect under the tested
all-position intervention. Do not call this an inherent flaw of all SAEs.

There is a position mismatch worth resolving before the report: SAE training and
graph construction use the final answer-prefix token, while the completed
held-out script used all token positions. `src.heldout_validation` now supports
`--positions last` (default remains `all` for backward compatibility).

The final justified GPU experiment is
`run_gpu_math_carry_feature_followup.ipynb`. It restores k=256 checkpoints and
the existing graph, runs final-token specificity, then calls
`src/math_carry_feature_screen.py`. The screen excludes the 12 inspected cases,
uses 8 fresh discovery pairs to rank all positive graph features by carry effect
minus no-carry effect, freezes that ranking, and evaluates a predeclared top-10
panel on 24 separate confirmation pairs. It also runs cumulative sizes, full
graph, bottom-ranked, per-layer and layer-count-matched random controls. The long
JSON is saved directly to Drive after each feature/panel and resumes safely.

The primary rule is fixed: mean paired difference below zero, bootstrap 95% CI
upper endpoint below zero, and mean carry-target delta below zero. If it fails,
do not select a secondary panel post hoc. Use the TopK sparsity gain and failed
specificity confirmation as a rigorous negative/partial reproduction.
